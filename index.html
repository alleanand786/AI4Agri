Description

Late blight, caused by the oomycete Phytophthora infestans, is a devastating disease of potatoes and tomatoes. It thrives in cool, moist conditions and can wipe out entire crops if left untreated.

Key Symptoms

  • 🔍Dark water-soaked lesions on leaves
  • 🔍White fuzzy growth on leaf undersides
  • 🔍Brown to black stem lesions
  • 🔍Rapid plant collapse

Treatment & Control

  • 🧪
    Fungicides
    Apply fungicides like mancozeb or chlorothalonil preventatively, especially during cool and wet weather.
  • 🍃
    Cultural Practices
    Ensure good air circulation by spacing plants properly. Remove and destroy infected plant debris.
  • 🔄
    Resistant Varieties
    Plant potato and tomato varieties that are resistant to late blight.
